## 2. THE PROBLEM

### Planning shouldn't be this much work.

**Paper planners** are simple — but they don't do anything with what you write.

You write a task.
Then you rewrite it tomorrow.
Then again next week.

You copy it onto the right date.
Then rewrite it in the weekly view.
Then again in the monthly.

You flip pages. You miss things. You rebuild your plan over and over.
And there are no reminders — so you need a separate app for that.

**Digital planners** can organize — but they aren't better.
They're slower.

Five taps to create one meeting. No free writing. No flexibility. No flow. And you still organize every view manually.

One note turns into five steps. One meeting turns into more work.

So you end up doing what most people do:
Managing your planner more than your actual life.

This isn't a discipline problem. It's a tool problem.

## PAGE NOTES

### Pre-launch vs. Post-launch

| Element | Pre-launch (Apr – Aug) | Post-launch (Aug+) |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Hero CTA | Email signup form | App Store download buttons |
| Pricing section | "Launching August 2026. Sign up to be first." | Full pricing + download buttons |
| Close CTA | Email signup | App Store download buttons |
| Nav bar | Logo + "Sign up" | Logo + "Download" |

### Design direction

- Warm linen background matching the app aesthetic
- Minimal design — lots of white space, premium feel
- No stock photos — only real app screenshots and GIFs
- GIFs auto-play, loop seamlessly, no controls
- Mobile-responsive — many visitors will come from LinkedIn on their phones
- Fast load — compress images, lazy-load below-the-fold content
- Email signup uses simple form: just email address + "Notify Me" button

### What NOT to include on the landing page

- No Kickstarter references
- No reward tiers or backer language
- No "coming soon" countdown timers (feels desperate)
- No feature comparison with named competitors (GoodNotes, Notion, etc.) — the comparison table uses categories, not brand names
- No technical details (SwiftUI, PencilKit, etc.) — save for press kit
